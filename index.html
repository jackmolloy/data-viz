GLOBAL BOX OFFICE

In 2016, the global box office for the film and movie industry reached $38.6 billion, its highest mark in history. Since being introduced in the early 20th century, motion films have established themselves as an integral part of the worldwide entertainment industry. They aren’t slowing down, either. The global film industry is projected to eclipse $49 billion by 2020, according to a 2016 Statista study.

Where the Money Is

Though the gross revenue average has exceeded the film industry’s budget average for the past decade, some years have been tighter than others. In 2008, average profit hit its decade-low $2,768,624. The margin reached its 10-year peak in 2015 with an average profit of $28,231,189 per movie.
